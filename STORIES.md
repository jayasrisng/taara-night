# Taara — all 88 enhanced bedtime stories

Each story is designed to be read in under two minutes and blends a gentle bedtime tale with one true detail about the constellation.

## 1. Ursa Minor — The Little Bear

Look what you found tonight: Ursa Minor, the Little Bear. The Little Bear wanders the northern sky, forever circling the still point of the sky. Her brightest star never moves, a gentle beacon for those who feel lost. Here is something true about it: Polaris sits at the end of its tail and lies very close to the north celestial pole, so northern travelers have long used it to find north. Now let your eyes grow heavy; the Little Bear will keep its gentle watch until morning.

## 2. Cassiopeia — The Queen's Throne

High above you tonight is Cassiopeia, the Queen's Throne. A queen sits upon her starry throne, shaped like a gentle W across the midnight canvas. She once boasted of her beauty, but the stars taught her not to brag. Astronomers know that its five brightest stars form an easy W or M, and because it is circumpolar from many northern places, it can remain visible all year. Rest your head, little stargazer.
